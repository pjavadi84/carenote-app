# Customer Interview Kit — Kinroster Business Validation

## Purpose

Desktop research (the five Perplexity prompts in April 2026) validated the market structurally. This kit executes the primary research step: 8-12 interviews with California RCFE owner-operators to answer the four open questions before committing full Phase 10 spend.

**The four questions these interviews must answer:**

1. Will a CA RCFE owner actually pay $299/mo for a voice-first + state-forms + family-portal SaaS? (Compare to $300 for ALIS or $400 for ECP.)
2. What is August Health's current CA penetration? (If 30%+ of 6-20 bed CA homes, the lane is closing. If <5%, we still have time.)
3. Which state compliance documents (LIC 611, LIC 624, LIC 9040, PMS forms) are operators paying someone else to prepare that Kinroster could automate?
4. What's the net-new wedge they'd pay for vs. their current EHR? Voice? Family portal? Incident reports? State-form automation?

## Target

- **5-8 completed interviews** if running this at $0 (zero-cash version, see below). **8-12 if you can scrape together $500-$1,200 for gift-card comp** (upgrade version).
- California RCFE owner-operators of 6-20 bed facilities.
- Mix of: 6-8 bed homes, 10-12 bed homes, 15-20 bed homes.
- Mix of: currently using ECP/ALIS/AL Advantage, and currently on paper/spreadsheets.
- Compensation: **$0 with peer-value trade (default)** or $75-$150 gift card per completed call (if budget allows).
- Timeline: **6-10 weeks at $0 comp**, 4-6 weeks at gift-card comp.
- Length: **30 minutes** at $0 comp (people donate less time for free), 45 minutes with gift card.

### Why zero-cash still works

Several well-known B2B SaaS products (Drift, Intercom early, Superhuman) did founder-led customer discovery without cash comp. The trade: response rate drops from ~25-40% (with gift cards) to ~10-15% without, and you get more self-selection toward operators who care about the product category. That's not necessarily bias — it's closer to your actual buyer persona.

What makes operators say yes to an unpaid call:

1. **Specificity in the outreach** — reference their Yelp reviews, their specific city, something they posted publicly. Proves you're not blasting 500 operators.
2. **Short ask** — 30 minutes, not 45. Lower bar to yes.
3. **Real peer value on offer** — first-access, free license, industry-insights summary they can't get elsewhere (see template below).
4. **Warm intros where possible** — friend-of-friend beats cold every time.
5. **Pre-research recovery** — you've lurked in the RCFE Owners Facebook group for 2-3 hours, so you can reference specific recent posts or pain points by name.

## Target list sources

In order of signal quality:

1. **CALA member directory** — https://www.caassistedliving.org. Paid membership but the directory is partially public. Best source for verified operators.
2. **CDSS facility search** — https://www.cdss.ca.gov/inforesources/community-care-licensing/facility-search-welcome. Public list of every licensed RCFE in California with contact info.
3. **RCFE Owners Network Facebook group** — https://www.facebook.com/groups/352285274494478/. 3K+ members, active since 2015. Post a recruiting ad.
4. **LinkedIn Sales Navigator** — filters: "RCFE", "assisted living administrator", "owner", location California, company size 1-10. Expect 30-40% connection-to-reply rate on cold outreach.
5. **Reddit r/smallbusiness + r/assistedliving** — post once per subreddit; low volume but occasional high-quality responses from operators actively venting.
6. **Local referral agents** — A Place for Mom, Caring.com regional advisors occasionally refer operators who are looking for software.

**Avoid:** LinkedIn InMail ads (low signal at this tier per research), cold Google-search scraping of facility websites (most don't have public contact info).

## Interview script (45 minutes, structured)

The goal is to learn, not to pitch. Do not show the product unless they explicitly ask.

### Before the call (5 min pre-work)

- Look up the facility on CDSS facility search — note bed count, license type, city, licensee name.
- Note which incumbents (ECP, ALIS, AL Advantage, August Health) dominate that size tier in that city, from research prompt 2.
- Set up the recording — Zoom with transcription, or Otter.ai standalone — and confirm consent verbally at the top.

### Opening (5 min)

1. **Intro.** "Thanks for making time. I'm [Name], founder of Kinroster. We're building documentation software for 6-20 bed RCFEs. This call is research, not a sales pitch — the product won't even come up unless you ask. I'll take notes. Do I have your permission to record this for my own reference? Your answers stay anonymous in any sharing."
2. **Their context.** "Tell me about your facility — how many beds, how long have you been running, ownership structure."

### Documentation workflow today (10 min)

3. "Walk me through how a caregiver documents a shift. From the moment they finish a task — like helping a resident with a meal — to where that note ends up."
4. "What fraction of shift notes are paper, digital, text-message, verbal handoff?"
5. "How do you prepare for a state survey? What do you have to assemble that isn't already in the right shape?"
6. "Which documents or forms do you spend the most time on — LIC 611, LIC 624, 9040, PMS?"

### Pain inventory (10 min)

7. "What's the single most broken thing about documentation at your facility?"
8. "Have you had any close calls or citations related to documentation in the last 2 years? What happened?"
9. "Family communication — how do you handle it? What do families complain about?"
10. "Incident reports — when something happens, walk me through how you get from the event to a finished report."

### Prior attempts (5 min)

11. "What software do you use today? How much per month, across how many tools?" **Listen specifically for: ALIS, ECP, AL Advantage, August Health, PointClickCare, MatrixCare, CareAcademy.**
12. "If you use [incumbent]: what works about it? What doesn't? Would you switch?"
13. "Have you tried voice tools or AI tools for documentation? What happened? Why did they stick or not stick?"

### Price discovery (10 min)

Show these three price points and read the hypothetical product description verbatim. Don't improvise the description.

> "Here's a hypothetical product: a voice-first documentation tool where caregivers describe each shift by talking to an AI, which turns their words into a structured note. It includes state-form automation for LIC 611 / 624, incident reporting, a family portal, and an audit trail. Month-to-month pricing, no annual contract."

14. "At $50 a month for your [bed count] facility — too cheap, about right, too expensive?"
15. "At $300 a month — too cheap, about right, too expensive?"
16. "At $600 a month — too cheap, about right, too expensive?"
17. "What would make the $300 price point clearly worth it?"
18. "What would make you walk away at any price?"

### Decision process + wrap (5 min)

19. "If you decided to try this, who else would need to approve?"
20. "What's your typical decision cycle for new software — a week, a month, a quarter?"
21. "What's your number-one compliance worry right now?"
22. "Have you heard of August Health? If yes — what's your impression?"
23. "Anything I should have asked but didn't?"
24. "Can I come back in 2-3 months if I have a demo ready to show you?"
25. Thank, send gift card within 24 hours, ask for referrals.

### After the call (within 2 hours)

- Upload recording + transcript to your secure notes folder.
- Fill in the tracking row (below) immediately while memory is fresh.
- **Gift-card version:** send the $100 gift card via email within 24 hours.
- **Zero-cash version:** send a thank-you email with an explicit hold date for the free first-access offer (e.g., "I'll email you when we open beta in [month]") and attach whatever interim findings you've compiled so far, even if rough. The peer-value trade is only real if you actually deliver.

## Synthesis template

After 8-12 interviews, compile into a one-page decision memo:

### TAM signal
- How many verified 6-20 bed RCFEs in the interviewees' network could they refer?
- Are there geographic pockets or ownership clusters that come up repeatedly?

### Price signal
- At $300/mo, what % of interviewees said "about right" or "too cheap"?
- At $300/mo, what's the bed-count distribution of yes vs. no?
- What's the median price point verbatim?

### Competitive signal
- How many had heard of August Health? What was the sentiment?
- Any other competitor that came up more than twice?

### Pain signal
- Top 3 pain points by frequency.
- Verbatim quotes for each (3 per pain point).

### Wedge signal
- Which of the four wedges (voice, state forms, family portal, audit) was most frequently cited as "most valuable"?
- Which was cited as a reason to switch incumbents?

### Decision (go / pivot / pause)

- **GO:** ≥60% said $300 is about right + ≤20% mentioned August Health as already using + pain is universal across interviews → commit Phase 10 spend.
- **PIVOT:** Price corridor collapses below $150/mo OR August Health penetration >30% → reposition (likely to state-forms-first or to larger facilities) before Phase 10.
- **PAUSE:** Neither demand nor competition nets clean → keep code assets warm, consider other verticals (outpatient mental health, SUD treatment, home health).

## Operational notes

- **Scheduling:** Calendly free tier is enough. Set 30-minute slots (or 45 for gift-card calls) with 15-min buffer. Offer 3 business days + 1 weekend slot per week.
- **Gift card delivery (if applicable):** Tremendous.com or Giftbit for programmatic sending. ~$0.50 fee per card. Sends via email within minutes.
- **Recording consent:** Always get verbal consent on the recording itself ("Do I have your permission to record…") AND offer an opt-out for their name being used in any public summary.
- **Follow-up:** After interview #3-5, compile a "halftime" summary and adjust the remaining calls if a pattern is emerging. Don't lock in all your interviews before listening.
- **Privacy:** Store recordings and transcripts in a password-protected folder (Notion, Dropbox encrypted, iCloud encrypted). Do not upload to any AI transcription service that hasn't signed a BAA — since you're not handling PHI (just adults talking about their business), HIPAA doesn't strictly apply, but operator names should stay confidential.

## Before you spend a single dollar or hour on interviews — free discovery

The cheapest research is research you can do without touching an operator. Do these first; they'll also sharpen your interview questions when you get to them.

### 1. RCFE Owners Network Facebook group lurking (2-3 hours, $0)

Read every post + comment in the group from the last 12 months. Note:
- Which software tools come up? How often? In what tone (complaint, recommendation, question)?
- What pain points repeat? What do they say when venting to peers they don't say to founders?
- Who are the loudest / most-engaged members? They're your warm-intro candidates later.

This is free. It gets you closer to the segment's real language than any amount of polished analyst report.

### 2. Reddit and subreddit archaeology (1-2 hours, $0)

- `r/smallbusiness` filter "RCFE" — real operators describing their business from the inside. The Nov 2025 thread "Operating small RCFEs in California completely changed how I think" is a gold mine (already cited in the Perplexity research).
- `r/assistedliving` — more caregiver voice than owner voice, but incident stories are telling.
- Quora threads on "how to run a 10-bed RCFE" — lower volume but occasional honesty.

### 3. CALA Elevate trade show as a visitor (1 day, $500-$700)

CALA's spring show is in June (Sacramento). Visitor-only tickets let you walk the floor without exhibiting. You get:
- 20+ casual hallway conversations with owners in one day.
- Direct eyes on what August Health, ECP, and ALIS are showing.
- A sense of which regional chains are consolidating at what pace.

This is 5x cheaper per-conversation than gift-card interviews and doesn't require scheduling. If you can swing a single investment in this whole research phase, this is where I'd put it ahead of gift cards.

### 4. Mystery shopping August Health, ECP, and ALIS (1 hour, $0)

Submit a "request a demo" form to each as a fictional 10-bed RCFE in Los Angeles. You'll learn:
- Actual pricing they quote to a small-home lead
- How aggressive their sales follow-up is
- What their positioning language sounds like

Use a non-personal email (kinroster-research@[something] rather than your real name) and politely decline if pressed for a call. Ethically grey — some people find it sketchy — so use judgment.

### 5. Build in public on LinkedIn + Twitter (ongoing, $0)

Post weekly about specific RCFE operational problems you're noticing. Tag the category, not specific people. Within 4-8 weeks, the right operators will DM you saying "actually that's exactly my problem." Those are warm leads for interviews that convert 4-5x better than cold outreach.

## What this research will NOT answer

- **Unit economics at scale.** CAC/LTV numbers can only be validated in the first 6-12 months of paid acquisition.
- **Regulatory appetite in other states.** These interviews focus CA; extrapolation to TX, FL is rough.
- **August Health's internal pricing ladder.** You'll get customer-side perception, not their actual GTM terms. A separate "mystery shopper" inquiry as a fake lead can fill this in later if needed.

## Decision deadline

**Target at zero-cash:** 10 weeks from first outreach to a Phase 10 go/pivot/pause decision. (Six weeks if you add gift-card comp.)

If you hit week 10 without at least 5 completed interviews in the zero-cash version:
- Add gift cards if you can now scrape together $500-$1K (often easier once you've had early conversations and can pitch a "product validation" budget to a friend investor).
- Or switch to a paid recruitment service (Respondent.io, UserInterviews.com have healthcare panels) — typically $75-$150 per recruited call, which is about the same as paying gift cards direct but with faster scheduling.
- Or commit to Phase 10 with less validation than ideal. Acceptable if the FB-lurking + mystery-shopping + 3-5 interviews have surfaced a clear signal; risky if the picture is still muddy.

Don't drift past week 14 — the competitive landscape moves, and August Health doesn't pause while you research.

## Sequencing recommendation for a zero-cash founder

Week 1-2:
- FB group lurking (section 1 above)
- Reddit archaeology (section 2)
- Mystery-shop August Health + ECP + ALIS (section 4)
- Start LinkedIn + Twitter posting (section 5)
- Draft and send first 10 outreach emails using the zero-cash template

Week 3-4:
- Conduct first 2-3 interviews as they schedule
- Iterate outreach copy based on reply patterns
- Attend CALA Elevate if budget permits (section 3)

Week 5-8:
- Target 5-8 total interviews
- Halftime synthesis after interview 3
- Keep posting in public to attract inbound

Week 9-10:
- Final synthesis
- Go / pivot / pause decision
- If go: start counsel-search outreach the same week

Total cash out-of-pocket if everything runs this way: **~$500-700** (CALA visitor ticket) or **$0** if you skip CALA. That's the real floor for this validation phase.
