# Kinroster — Go-to-Market Strategy

## GTM Philosophy

Kinroster's early growth is relationship-driven, not ad-driven. Small care home operators trust people they've met, operators they've talked to, and communities they belong to. The GTM strategy matches this: high-touch, community-first, earned trust.

## Channel Strategy (Ranked by Expected Conversion)

### Tier 1: Highest Conversion — Do These First

#### 1. State RCFE / Adult Care Home Associations

Every state has an association for residential care operators. These are the single highest-density channel for our ICP.

**Actions:**
- Join as a vendor/associate member (~$200–500/year)
- Offer to present at a monthly meeting: "How AI Can Cut Documentation Time in Half"
- Ask the association director to introduce you to 5 operators who struggle with paperwork
- Sponsor the association newsletter ($100–300/issue)

**Key associations to target first:**
- California: CALA (California Assisted Living Association), RCFE-specific regional groups
- Washington: Adult Family Home Council
- Oregon: Oregon Health Care Association
- Texas: Texas Assisted Living Association
- Florida: Florida Assisted Living Association

**Expected outcome:** 3–5 warm introductions per association, 30–50% conversion to pilot.

#### 2. Facebook Groups

Small care home operators are active on Facebook, not LinkedIn, Twitter, or Reddit.

**Target groups (search for these):**
- "RCFE Owners" / "RCFE Owners California"
- "Adult Family Home Owners"
- "Board and Care Owners"
- "[State] Residential Care Home Owners"
- "Home Care Agency Owners"

These groups typically have 2,000–15,000 members.

**Strategy:**
- Join and observe for 1 week — understand the tone, common complaints, rules
- Post genuinely: ask about documentation pain, share useful tips, don't pitch
- Respond to posts where operators complain about paperwork, surveys, or family communication
- After building credibility (2–3 weeks), offer to let a few operators try Kinroster for free
- Operators in these groups refer each other constantly — one happy user becomes 3–5 referrals

**Do NOT:** Post promotional content, use sales language, or violate group rules. You'll be removed.

#### 3. Direct Outreach via State Licensing Databases

Most states publish lists of licensed care facilities with owner names and addresses. These are free, complete, and public.

**Where to find them:**
- California: CDSS Community Care Licensing website
- Texas: HHSC provider search
- Washington: DSHS residential care services
- Florida: AHCA facility locator

**Strategy:**
- Pull facility lists for your metro area
- Send a personalized postcard or handwritten note (these operators get very little mail from tech companies — it stands out)
- Follow up with a phone call 3–5 days later
- Offer a free demo and 14-day trial

**Expected outcome:** 5–10% response rate on postcards, 20–30% of responses convert to demo.

### Tier 2: Good for Co-Founders, Investors, and Partnerships

#### 4. LinkedIn (Co-Founders and Investors Only)

Facilities aren't on LinkedIn. But potential co-founders and investors are.

**People to search for:**
- Former Directors of Nursing who left clinical work for entrepreneurship
- Geriatric care managers running solo practices
- People who've worked at CareSmartz, Honor, Home Instead corporate, or senior living REITs
- Angel investors with healthcare or aging-tech portfolios
- People with "elder care" or "aging" in their LinkedIn headline

**Strategy:**
- Connect with a personalized message referencing their background
- Share what you're building and why
- Ask for 15 minutes of their time for feedback (not a pitch)

#### 5. Co-Founder Matching Platforms

| Platform | Best For |
|----------|---------|
| **YC Co-Founder Matching** | High-quality technical and business co-founders; free |
| **Indie Hackers** | Solo founder community; good for accountability and peer feedback |
| **AngelList** | Investor connections; co-founder matching |

**Ideal co-founder profile:** Someone who has operated a care home or managed caregivers. They bring domain credibility, pilot facilities from their network, and product intuition you can't build from the outside.

**Equity consideration:** A domain co-founder is worth 10–20% equity. They are your credibility, your first tester, and your sales channel.

#### 7. Google Business Outreach

## Pilot Acquisition Strategy

### Goal: 5 Pilot Facilities in 30 Days

| Week | Action | Target |
|------|--------|--------|
| Week 1 | Join 3 Facebook groups, post introduction | Identify 10 interested operators |
| Week 1 | Join state association, request introductions | 3–5 warm leads |
| Week 2 | Phone calls to 20 local facilities from licensing database | 5 demos scheduled |
| Week 2 | DM engaged Facebook group members | 3 additional demos |
| Week 3 | Demo Kinroster to interested operators | 8–10 demos total |
| Week 4 | Onboard first 5 facilities with free lifetime access | 5 active pilots |

### Pilot Incentive Structure

| Incentive | Offer | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| **Free lifetime access** | First 5 facilities | In exchange for weekly 15-min feedback calls and a testimonial |
| **Referral commission** | 20% of first-year revenue per referred facility | Paid monthly; capped at 24 months |
| **No equity to facilities** | Do not offer | Creates legal complexity and misaligned incentives |

### What Pilots Commit To

- Use Kinroster for all shift notes for at least 4 residents
- Provide 15-minute weekly feedback call for the first 8 weeks
- Allow anonymized usage data for product improvement
- Provide a written testimonial or case study if satisfied

## Early Sales Motion

### For First 10 Customers (Founder-Led Sales)

1. **Discovery call** (15 min): Learn about their facility, documentation pain, current tools
2. **Live demo** (20 min): Show Kinroster with their actual resident scenario (use a made-up resident during demo)
3. **Trial setup** (10 min): Help them add their first 3 residents and enter a note
4. **Check-in call** (15 min, day 3): How's it going? What's confusing?
5. **Check-in call** (15 min, day 7): Review structured notes, gather feedback
6. **Close** (day 10–14): Convert trial to paid subscription

### Key Sales Messages

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "I don't have time to learn new software" | "Enter your first note in 30 seconds. I'll show you right now on your phone." |
| "My caregivers won't use it" | "If they can text, they can use Kinroster. The notes they type here are shorter than a text message." |
| "How is this different from ChatGPT?" | "ChatGPT doesn't remember your residents, doesn't store your notes, and won't pass a state survey. Kinroster is built specifically for care documentation." |
| "Is it HIPAA compliant?" | "We follow healthcare data security best practices. All data is encrypted, access-controlled, and never shared. We're pursuing formal HIPAA certification." |
| "What if the AI makes a mistake?" | "Every note is shown to the caregiver before saving. Nothing goes to families without the manager reviewing it first. You're always in control." |
