# Foundational LinkedIn post — pin this

**When to post:** Monday morning Pacific time (best engagement for healthcare ops).
**Pin it:** to your profile. Every cold outreach links back to it.
**Tag (once, not spammy):** CALA, Argentum, NCAL (US). 長照協會 (Taiwan, when you do that motion).
**Add an image:** a screenshot of John Smith's structured shift note (with John Smith / facility name redacted to "Resident A" / "Sunrise Care Home"). Visual content gets 2× the engagement.

---

## Variant A — "story-led" (recommended)

> I spent the last year watching caregivers at small residential care homes spend 15 minutes after every shift writing notes by hand — and then watched those notes get filed in a binder no family member or doctor ever sees.
>
> The owners I talked to weren't worried about AI or fancy software. They were worried about their next state audit, families calling to ask "how is mom doing today?", and caregivers burning out from paperwork.
>
> So I built Kinroster. A caregiver speaks for 90 seconds about a resident's shift — and an AI turns that into a structured, audit-ready note. Families get an opt-in update without the admin lifting a finger. The resident's doctor can pull up a summary before a visit instead of guessing.
>
> No paperwork tax, no families left in the dark, no documentation nightmares the night before an audit.
>
> If you run a small RCFE (6–20 beds) and want a free pilot — DM me. I'm only working with 5 facilities this round and I'd love yours to be one of them.

**Why this works:**
- Opens with a concrete scene, not a feature pitch
- Names the buyer's *real* pains (audit, family calls, caregiver burnout) before the product
- "No paperwork tax" is the line they'll remember
- Scarcity (5 facilities) drives the DM
- Sounds human, not marketing-spoken

## Tips when posting

1. **Don't link out in the post body.** LinkedIn punishes posts with outbound links. Put the link in a first comment ("More at kinroster.com").
2. **Add one image** (screenshot of structured note, redacted) — 2× engagement.
3. **Reply to every comment in the first 24 hours.** That's where the LinkedIn algo decides whether to keep pushing the post.
4. **Don't edit after posting for at least 60 minutes** — edits in the first hour kill reach.
5. **Tag two people, not ten.** Choose two people likely to comment (a CALA exec, a respected care home owner you've talked to). Mass-tagging triggers spam filters.

## Follow-up post idea (week 2 or 3)

Once you have one pilot, post a short "what I learned in week 1 of pilot" with one concrete metric (e.g., "caregivers documented 87% of shifts vs. 41% before" — make sure the number is real). That's the post that converts skeptics from the foundational post.
